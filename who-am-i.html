Who am I and how do we work

Hi, I'm Magda.

Originally from Georgia, I have been living in Hungary for more than five years. I am currently completing my PhD in Clinical Psychology and Addiction Science at ELTE, while specializing in evidence-based Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Schema Therapy.

The process of therapy with me is very collaborative.

My main priority is creating a space where you feel completely accepted just as you are; no masks, no performance, and no filters.

In my opinion, what makes my practice a bit different is that one of my highest values in life is authenticity, and I bring that directly into our sessions. I am simply incapable of sitting across from you with a completely neutral, blank mask. My face reflects my genuine reactions; whether that means showing raw empathy, sharing your surprise, laughing with you, or visibly reacting to something unfair that happened to you. I am right there in the experience with you.

Within this authentic and secure environment, we utilize the following:

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

This is my primary approach. It is an evidence-based therapy focused on the present, helping you identify and shift negative thinking habits into actionable, positive changes for a more balanced daily life.

Schema Therapy

For patterns that run deeper, I integrate Schema Therapy. This is an integrative psychotherapy that combines elements of cognitive behavioral, psychodynamic, and emotion-focused therapies. It targets deep-rooted, long-standing emotional patterns—often originating from unmet childhood needs—that cause recurring life and relationship struggles today.

Psychoeducation

I believe that understanding the why behind your behavior is incredibly powerful. By giving you clear, science-backed insights into how your brain, body, and emotions work, you gain the knowledge you need to become the expert on your own mental health.
