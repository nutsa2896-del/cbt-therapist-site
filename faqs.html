Frequently Asked Questions

Think of our first session as a casual, collaborative test run rather than a deep dive. We won't go too deep right away; instead, we'll look at the main challenges you are facing and what you hope to get out of therapy. Most importantly, it's a chance to see if we click, because a strong, comfortable relationship between us is the real foundation for all the work we'll do.

The relationship between a psychologist and a client is a very important factor in your journey. In reality, no psychologist is the perfect match for every single person, and that is completely normal!

If we have a few sessions (or maybe even right after the first one) and you feel like my style, personality, or approach isn't the right fit for you, please let me know. This goes both ways, too; if I feel that my specific expertise or approach isn't the absolute best match for what you need, I will be honest with you about it. Your well-being is the top priority, and I will gladly help you connect with another trusted professional who might be a better match.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is an evidence-based, practical approach that focuses on the present. Together, we look at your day-to-day thinking habits and behaviors. If you are stuck in negative thought loops or chronic anxiety, CBT helps us break those patterns down into manageable pieces and turn them into actionable, positive changes for a more balanced life.

Learn more about CBT →

Schema Therapy looks at patterns that run a bit deeper. It is an integrative approach that helps us identify long-standing emotional traps (or "schemas") that usually originate from unmet childhood needs. If you find yourself constantly stuck in the same stubborn relationship patterns, career struggles, or self-sabotaging habits, Schema Therapy helps us understand why you get triggered and how to break the cycle.
