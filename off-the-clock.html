Off the clock

Honestly, I don't have it all figured out. Being a psychologist doesn't mean I'm immune to life's messes; it just means I am constantly using the very same tools I teach to navigate my own world.

When I am not wrapped up in research, academia, or working with clients, you can usually find me traveling. I especially love solo traveling because it intentionally throws me into new, unpredictable situations. Does it always go smoothly? Absolutely not. Sometimes things fail spectacularly, but that is exactly why I do it; it forces me to test my own limits, figure things out in the moment, and keep learning about myself.

But my ultimate reset button? Dancing. I love to dance a lot. When the week gets heavy, there is nothing better than going out dancing with my friends. It is the best way I know to completely quiet my overactive, analytical mind, step out of my head, and just be present.

So, if you ever wonder what your psychologist is doing on the weekend: I'm likely somewhere in a new country, navigating a missed train, or on a dance floor with my friends, just doing my best to live life fully, flaws and all.
