For Expats

Moving abroad is a massive achievement, but it can also carry an invisible psychological weight.

On paper, you are navigating a new culture, advancing your career, and building an exciting lifestyle. On the inside, you might be dealing with the hidden isolation, professional burnout, loneliness, or the exhaustion of reshaping what "home" means. You don't have to untangle this transition alone.

A Psychologist Who Shares Your Journey

As an expat myself and a PhD researcher working in a multicultural academic setting, I don't just understand the clinical theory of cultural adjustment, I live it every day.

I know firsthand what it feels like to build a life from scratch, to balance conflicting cultural contexts, and to carry the unique weight of international career pressure.

In our sessions, we will work practically and directly to help you navigate these transitions, manage stress, and build a meaningful, grounded life where you are today.
